Validation of Linear Advection

1. Implemented an analytical comparison.
2. Initial validation showed large error spikes at the boundaries.
3. This was due to analytical solution not having the same periodicity as the numerical solution. (Numerical progressed 5x faster)
4. After correcting this, the spikes dissapeared.
5. Remaining error is a numerical artifact.

6. Interestingly, the upwind operator displayed lower error than the gradient.
7. Central gradient preserves energy perfectly, but introduces dispersive errors that break down the shape, leading to higher L2 and relative error.
8. Upwind loses energy but maintains a smooth pulse, hence has relatively lower error.
9. Conclusion: Gradient --> dispersive, Upwind --> Dissapative
